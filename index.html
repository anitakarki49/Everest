What is Pure Shilajit and How is it Made?

Shilajit is a substance that forms between layers of rock in high altitude areas of the Himalayas and Everest Region over a long period of time by the degradation of plant humus. It contains a variety of complex organic compounds such as fulvic and humic acids, plant alkaloids, and trace minerals. During the summer, the dark liquid oozes from the rocks and is harvested, but it may contain impurities like stones and sand. To remove these impurities, the raw shilajit is mixed with spring water and then dried to become a pure shilajit extract.
